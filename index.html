Who We Are

A Hebrew Roots Congregation

✦
✦ Est. 2011 · EIN: 35-2924426

Yahweh's House of Prayer is a Hebrew Roots ministry led by ordained ministers with over 20 years of teaching experience. Our mission is to restore authentic first-century biblical faith — as taught by Yeshua and the early Jewish believers — rejecting later ecclesiastical corruptions and Hellenistic distortions of the faith.

We follow the ways of the early Ebionite and Nazarene movements, honoring the sacred Name of Yahweh, observing His Torah, and keeping His appointed Feasts as commanded in scripture. We are a congregation rooted in covenant identity, reaching across Racine, Wisconsin; Natchez, Mississippi; and Atlanta, Georgia — and serving a growing global online congregation.

Yahweh's House of Prayer operates in covenant partnership with the Global Kingdom of Yehudah — a faith-based micro-nation recognizing the covenant identity of Yisrael — and with Infiniti Diviniti Habitats, our housing NGO serving veterans, foster youth, and the vulnerable in our communities.

✦ What We Believe

  • Yahweh is the one true Elohim — His Name matters
  • Yeshua is the Hebrew Messiah — not a Roman deity
  • Torah was not abolished — it is our covenant walk
  • The seventh-day Shabbat is eternal and binding
  • Leviticus 23 Feasts — not Christmas or Easter
  • The full canon including Ethiopian Orthodox scripture

✦ Our Services

  • Shabbat Services (Friday eve – Saturday eve)
  • Hebrew Language & Bible Study Classes
  • Baptism, Marriages & Ordinations
  • Jail & Prison Ministry (MS Dept. of Corrections)
  • Housing & Community Assistance Programs
  • Charity Division — food, clothing & shelter

What We Believe

Our Doctrinal Foundation — The Ancient Paths

✦
"Thus says Yahweh: Stand at the crossroads and look; ask for the ancient paths, ask where the good way is, and walk in it, and you will find rest for your souls." — Yirmeyahu (Jeremiah) 6:16
𐤉𐤄𐤅𐤄

The Name of Yahweh

We believe in the one true Elohim whose Name is Yahweh. His Name was intentionally removed from scripture over 6,000 times and replaced with "LORD." We restore and honor His Name as revealed to Moshe at the burning bush — because a name is not a title, and Yahweh declared His Name as a memorial forever.

"I am Yahweh — that is My Name." — Yeshayahu 42:8
📜

Torah is Eternal

Yahweh's Torah (instruction) was not abolished. Yeshua himself said he came not to destroy the Torah but to fulfill it. The Greek word "plēroō" means to complete or make full — not to eliminate. We walk in Torah obedience as the covenant lifestyle Yahweh commanded for all who love Him.

"Do not think I came to abolish the Torah." — Mattityahu 5:17
🕎

Yeshua the Messiah

We believe Yeshua — not "Jesus," a Greek/Latin rendering — was the promised Hebrew Messiah, a Torah-observant teacher from Natzeret. He came to restore Yisrael and teach true covenant living. We follow his actual teachings as recorded in the Hebraic gospel tradition, not the Hellenized Roman version.

"I was sent only to the lost sheep of the house of Yisrael." — Mattityahu 15:24
✡️

Hebrew Roots, Not Greek

The entire Bible was written by Hebrews, for Hebrews, in a Hebrew cultural and linguistic context. The Greek church imposed foreign philosophical frameworks — Platonic dualism, pagan festivals, and Roman hierarchy — onto Hebrew scripture. We read the Word through Hebrew eyes, culture, and language.

"Salvation is from the Yehudim." — Yochanan 4:22
🌙

Biblical Holy Days

We observe the seven Biblical Feasts of Yahweh as commanded in Vayikra (Leviticus) 23 — Pesach, Chag HaMatzot, Yom HaBikkurim, Shavuot, Yom Teruah, Yom Kippur, and Sukkot. These are not "Jewish holidays" — they are Yahweh's appointed times, eternal in their covenant significance.

"These are My appointed feasts." — Vayikra 23:2
🕯️

Seventh-Day Shabbat

We honor the Shabbat from Friday evening to Saturday evening as Yahweh commanded at Creation and reaffirmed in Torah. Shabbat was never changed to Sunday — that was a Roman decree under Emperor Constantine in 321 CE. The fourth commandment has never been repealed by Yahweh.

"Remember the Shabbat day to keep it holy." — Shemot 20:8
🦁

Identity of Yisrael

We affirm the covenant identity of the Tribes of Yisrael and the importance of understanding one's ancestral and spiritual heritage. Our ministry acknowledges the lineage of the Tribe of Benyamin and the broader scattered house of Yisrael being gathered back to covenant in these last days.

"I will gather the remnant of Yisrael." — Mikah 2:12
📖

The Full Canon

We recognize the broader canon of scripture including the Ethiopian Orthodox Bible (81 books), the Book of Enoch, the Book of Jubilees, and the Dead Sea Scrolls as valuable ancient witnesses to the Hebrew faith — texts the Roman church deliberately excluded from the Western canon.

"All scripture is given by inspiration of Yahweh." — 2 Timotheos 3:16
🙏

Prayer & Intercession

Prayer is direct communication with Yahweh through Yeshua the Messiah. We call upon Yahweh by His true Name. We intercede for the congregation, for the scattered house of Yisrael, and for all who are seeking the ancient paths. Prayer, fasting, and the study of His Word are the pillars of our faith walk.

"My house shall be called a house of prayer." — Yeshayahu 56:7
🏛️

Early Church Restoration

We look to the Ebionite and Nazarene movements — the earliest Hebrew followers of Yeshua — as the model for authentic faith. These communities maintained Torah observance, used Hebrew names, and preserved the original apostolic teaching before Rome imposed its institutional church structure.

"Contend earnestly for the faith delivered to the saints." — Yehudah 1:3
⚖️

Justice & Care for the Poor

Torah commands us to care for the poor, the orphan, the widow, and the stranger. Our housing ministry — Infiniti Diviniti Habitats — and our Charity Division are the practical expression of Yahweh's justice. Faith without works is dead; we serve our community as an act of covenant faithfulness.

"Defend the poor and fatherless; do justice to the afflicted." — Tehillim 82:3
🌍

Global Kingdom Vision

Yahweh's House of Prayer operates in partnership with the Global Kingdom of Yehudah — a faith-based covenant nation recognizing the spiritual and ancestral identity of Yisrael among all peoples. This kingdom is not of this world's political systems; it is a covenant community aligned with the Kingdom of Yahweh.

"Yahweh shall be King over all the earth." — Zekharyah 14:9

Holy Days of Yahweh

The Appointed Times — Vayikra (Leviticus) 23

✦

"These are the appointed feasts of Yahweh, the holy convocations, which you shall proclaim at the time appointed for them." — Vayikra 23:4

Nisan14–21

Feast of Unleavened Bread

Chag HaMatzot · חַג הַמַּצּוֹת

Seven days of removing leaven — a picture of removing sin and walking in the purity of Yahweh's covenant. Begins the evening of Passover.

Spring
Nisan16

Firstfruits

Yom HaBikkurim · יוֹם הַבִּכּוּרִים

The waving of the first of the harvest before Yahweh. Yeshua rose on this very day as the firstfruit of the resurrection — fulfilling this appointed time.

Spring
Sivan6

Feast of Weeks

Shavuot · שָׁבוּעוֹת

Fifty days after Firstfruits — celebrating the giving of the Torah at Mount Sinai and the outpouring of the Ruach HaKodesh in Acts 2.

Spring
Tishri1

Feast of Trumpets

Yom Teruah · יוֹם תְּרוּעָה

The Day of Blowing — a day of awakening, the sound of the shofar calling Yisrael to repentance and foreshadowing the return of the Messiah.

Fall
Tishri10

Day of Atonement

Yom Kippur · יוֹם כִּפּוּר

The holiest day of the year — a day of fasting, affliction of soul, and standing before Yahweh for cleansing and atonement of sin.

High Holy Day
Tishri15–21

Feast of Tabernacles

Sukkot · סֻכּוֹת

Seven days of dwelling in booths — remembering Yahweh's provision in the wilderness and looking forward to His dwelling with His people forever.

Fall
Tishri22

Eighth Day Assembly

Shemini Atzeret · שְׁמִינִי עֲצֶרֶת

The solemn closing assembly after Sukkot — a day set apart for Yahweh alone, pointing to eternity and the completion of all things in His Kingdom.

Fall
