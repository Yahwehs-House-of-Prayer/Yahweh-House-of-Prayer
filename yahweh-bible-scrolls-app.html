The Queen of Sheba

Makeda · Mother of Menelik I · Keeper of the Ark

✦ Her Story in the Ancient Texts

The Queen of Sheba — known in Ethiopian tradition as Makeda — was a powerful sovereign ruler who heard of the wisdom of Shlomo (Solomon) and traveled to Yerushalayim to test him with hard questions. Her visit is recorded in Melachim Aleph (1 Kings 10) and Divrei HaYamim Bet (2 Chronicles 9) of the Hebrew Tanakh.

The Kebra Nagast ("Glory of Kings"), the sacred Ethiopian text, reveals the fuller story: Makeda converted to the faith of Yahweh, bore Shlomo's son Menelik I, and through Menelik, the Ark of the Covenant was brought to Ethiopia — where Ethiopian Orthodox tradition holds it remains to this day in Axum.

1 Kings 10 2 Chronicles 9 Kebra Nagast Ethiopian Orthodox Bible Quran 27:22-44

✦ The Ethiopian Connection & the Ark

According to the Kebra Nagast, when Menelik I came of age, he traveled to meet his father Shlomo in Yerushalayim. The priests' sons, including Azariah (son of Zadok the High Priest), accompanied Menelik back to Ethiopia — and took with them the Ark of the Covenant.

This explains why Ethiopia maintained one of the oldest continuous Hebrew-rooted faith traditions in the world — the Beta Israel (Ethiopian Jews) and the Ethiopian Orthodox Tewahedo Church, which still observes Shabbat, keeps dietary laws, and holds 81 books in its sacred canon — including texts lost to the Western church.
