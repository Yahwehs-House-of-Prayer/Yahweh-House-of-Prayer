Prayer & Worship
Shabbat liturgy · Hebrew prayers · Psalms of Yahweh

The Shema

שְׁמַע יִשְׂרָאֵל יַהְוֶה אֱלֹהֵינוּ יַהְוֶה אֶחָד

"Hear, O Yisrael: Yahweh our Elohim, Yahweh is One. And you shall love Yahweh your Elohim with all your heart, with all your soul, and with all your strength."

Devarim (Deuteronomy) 6:4–5 · The Foundation of Faith

Shabbat Candle Blessing

בָּרוּךְ אַתָּה יַהְוֶה

"Blessed are You, Yahweh our Elohim, King of the universe, who has sanctified us with His commandments and commanded us to kindle the Shabbat light."

Friday Evening · Shabbat Begins at Sundown

Kiddush — Sanctification

וַיְכֻלּוּ הַשָּׁמַיִם וְהָאָרֶץ

"And the heavens and the earth were finished, and all the host of them. And on the seventh day Yahweh finished His work that He had done, and He rested on the seventh day..."

Bereshit (Genesis) 2:1–3 · Shabbat Evening Kiddush

Havdalah — Separation Prayer

הַמַּבְדִּיל בֵּין קֹדֶשׁ לְחֹל

"Blessed is Yahweh who separates between the holy and the ordinary, between light and darkness, between Yisrael and the nations, between the seventh day and the six days of creation."

Saturday Evening · Shabbat Ends · Week Begins

Psalm 119 — Torah Meditation

אַשְׁרֵי תְמִימֵי דָרֶךְ הַהֹלְכִים בְּתוֹרַת יַהְוֶה

"How blessed are those whose way is blameless, who walk in the Torah of Yahweh. How blessed are those who keep His testimonies, who seek Him with all their heart."

Tehillim (Psalms) 119:1–2 · The Torah Psalm
